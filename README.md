## Featured Projects

### 1. Smart PUC — Blockchain-Based Vehicle Emission Monitoring System
`Python` `FastAPI` `Solidity` `Ethereum` `Blockchain` `AI/ML` `IoT Concepts`

**Problem Statement**
Vehicle emission testing (PUC) in its current form is largely manual, paper-based, and prone to fraud and certificate manipulation, making large-scale pollution compliance monitoring difficult for regulatory authorities.

**Architecture Overview**
The system is designed around a FastAPI backend that interfaces with Ethereum smart contracts (written in Solidity) to record emission test results immutably. IoT-based sensor concepts simulate real-time emission data capture, while an AI-assisted analysis layer flags anomalies and potential fraud patterns before data is committed to the blockchain.

**Key Features**
- Tamper-proof, blockchain-based digital PUC certification
- Smart contract-driven validation of emission test results
- AI-assisted fraud and anomaly detection in submitted data
- Conceptual IoT integration for real-time emission monitoring
- Transparent compliance tracking accessible to regulatory stakeholders

**Learning Outcomes**
Gained hands-on experience integrating blockchain (Solidity/Ethereum) with a traditional backend (FastAPI), understanding smart contract design, and applying AI techniques to support fraud detection within a regulatory compliance use case.

### 2. V2V Communication for Blind Spot Detection
`Research` `Physics-Based Modelling` `AI Hybrid Systems`

**Problem Statement**
Blind spots remain a significant contributor to vehicle collisions, particularly in dense traffic conditions where conventional sensor-based detection systems face range and obstruction limitations.

**Proposed Solution**
This research project proposes a Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) communication framework that combines physics-based motion modelling with AI-driven hybrid systems to assess collision risk in real time, enabling vehicles to share positional and velocity data directly with one another.

**Key Concepts**
- Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) communication protocols
- Physics-based modelling for motion and risk prediction
- Hybrid AI systems combining rule-based and learned models
- Real-time collision risk assessment

**Learning Outcomes**
Strengthened research and analytical skills through literature review, mathematical modelling of vehicle dynamics, and exploration of how AI hybrid systems can be applied to safety-critical real-time decision-making problems.
